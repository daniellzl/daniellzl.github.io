ABOUT ME

I've been learning about computer science and building web applications for almost two years now. I started my journey by completing the famed CS50 introduction to computer science course hosted by HarvardX. Having thoroughly enjoyed learning about how computers are used to solve a wide range real-word problems, I decided to pursue software development as a career.In order to grow my programming skills, I took on the challenge of building all the development projects assigned by freeCodeCamp's excellent curriculum. I also taught myself the fundamentals of data structures and algorithms in order to solve more advanced computing problems.

Most of my current time is spent working on Node.js applications as a software engineer at Peerbuds Innovation Lab. I also help run Peerbuds iLab's coding interview question study group.

EDUCATION

I graduated from U.C. Berkeley with a B.A. in Applied Mathematics. In regards to computing, I have completed Harvard's Introduction to CS on edX and finished the front-end and back-end portions of freeCodeCamp's curriculum.

At the current moment, my attention is spread across a few different pursuits. I was lucky enough to be exposed to the world of Blockchain and Ethereum by happenstance and spend most of my free time exploring this domain. I am also learning D3.js to round out my web development skills. As a long term goal, I am also slowly going through MIT's undergraduate computer science curriculum to improve my knowledge.

SKILLS

I can build a full-stack web application using the Node.js framework. I am familiar with React.js on the front-end.
